Top 10 most innovative companies in Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality of 2023

BY Lesly Ann Garachico
Long read

Explore the full 2023 list of Fast Company’s Most Innovative Companies, featuring 540 organizations that are reshaping industries and culture. We’ve highlighted the firms making the biggest impact across 54 categories, including artificial intelligence, augmented and virtual reality, gaming, and more.

The metaverse—and by extension, the mixed-reality headgear that might be used to access it—had a moment in the spotlight in 2021. This surge in interest was partly due to the social isolation of the pandemic and partly due to Mark Zuckerberg’s decision to rebrand Facebook as Meta, betting on a future immersed in virtual spaces. However, much of the excitement around an immersive virtual public space has faded, as it became clear that the hardware, software, and standards necessary to realize this vision are not yet fully developed.

As public interest shifted, consumer enthusiasm for augmented and virtual reality seemed to cool. In the U.S., for example, sales of VR headsets declined by 2% year-over-year as of early December 2022, following a year of doubling sales in 2021, according to research from NPD Group. Nonetheless, tech companies—both large and small—continue to invest substantial resources and top talent into spatial computing devices and experiences.

Despite incurring a $10 billion loss on its Reality Labs business in 2021, Meta has indicated that it plans to continue spending at a similar rate to advance its VR, AR, and metaverse aspirations. The company set a new benchmark in VR headsets with its Quest Pro, which features enhanced pass-through imaging, new eye-tracking technology, and improved hand controllers.

1. Unity

Unity has pioneered digital twin technology, helping organizations like Vancouver Airport Authority and the Orlando Economic Partnership visualize operations, train personnel, and plan with data-driven simulations.

2. Coca-Cola

Coca-Cola's campaign, Coca-Cola Creations, introduced mysterious flavors like "Starlight" and "Dreamworld" with AR-enhanced experiences, setting a trend for brand engagement via augmented reality.

3. Meta

Meta launched the Quest Pro headset, offering improved "pass-through" imaging, eye-tracking, and enhanced mixed-reality capabilities, setting a new standard for VR headsets.

4. Google

Google's Project Starline brought 3D teleconferencing closer to reality, enabling remote communication with life-sized, lifelike representations and bridging the gap in virtual meetings.

5. Innersloth

Innersloth’s VR adaptation of *Among Us* delivers an intense first-person experience with spatial audio, enhancing the suspense and thrill of the original game.

6. Niantic

With its Lightship Virtual Positioning System, Niantic created a global AR map for anchoring digital objects in real-world locations, expanding interactive experiences beyond VR headsets.

7. Rendever

Rendever’s VR platform, including the RendeverFit program, enhances mental fitness and social interaction for seniors through immersive experiences, promoting mental well-being.

8. Archer’s Mark

Archer’s Mark created *On the Morning You Wake (to the End of the World)*, an impactful VR experience that lets users relive Hawaii’s 2018 nuclear false alarm, sparking reflection on nuclear risks.

9. Varjo

Varjo’s Reality Cloud technology allows enterprises to stream high-resolution XR content across devices, streamlining the XR collaboration experience for industries like automotive.

10. PatchXR

PatchXR's Patchworld brings virtual instruments into a 3D space, enabling users to create and mix electronic music in immersive environments on Meta Quest 2.
